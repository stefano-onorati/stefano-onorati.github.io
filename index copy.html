Bicycle Race

Interactive Racing Game | December 2013

Bicycle Race is an interactive racing game which focuses on the issue of PED use in cycling. The game utilizes the bicycle as the main controller for users to race in the game, which is connected to the computer through Arduino computing and communicates with Unity3D.

The objective is quite simple in Bicycle Race: the user bikes their way to victory and win the race. However, they are faced with a complex problem with significant consequences; in order to win the race, the user must take performing-enhancing drugs to gain a competitive advantage and increase the likelihood of winning the race. The user wins if they are not caught; otherwise, they must prepare for public scrutiny. However, if the user chooses not to ruin their reputation by taking drugs, they can race without giving in to peer pressure. This tackles the issue of PED use that is very prominent in the sport of cycling today, and presents the challenges these athletes face and fear about.

TOOLS USED: Unity, Javascript, Arduino

  • Authors: Stefano Onorati & Patrick Reding
  • Project Type: School

Improv Soirée Visualizer

Music Visualizer | November 2014

The Improv Soirée Visualizer was one of several music visualizers developed by students for the Improv Soirée at York University in November 2014. The visualizers were displayed live in front of a small audience, and visually complemented the improv music performances.

My version of the Improv Soirée Visualizer generates spheres of various sizes to signify volume and decaying in size over time, along with frequency lines above and below the spheres. The visualizer receives audio input and sends this information to Processing to change the shape of the spheres and frequency lines in the sketch. Using touchOSC, certain visual properties of the spheres and lines, such as colour, stroke, and decay rate, were altered during the live improv performances.

TOOLS USED: Processing, TouchOSC, Minim

  • Author: Stefano Onorati
  • Project Type: School

IVG Weather

Interactive Art | May 2015

The Interactive Video Gallery (IVG) Weather is an interactive art piece which displays weather information from various cities worldwide. Alongside Nabil and Will, we developed this project during the Spring term and created two components that worked off each other: the user interaction and the aesthetics.

The main interaction of this piece is through Twitter, using the hashtag #IVGYorku. Users can send a tweet using the hashtag and must include a city name. Once the tweet is processed in the program, a message is sent out to the visuals to update its aesthetics. Relevant information displayed through visuals include time of day, temperature, and wind direction and speed.

Tools Used: Processing, Twitter4j, YahooWeather, OSCP5

  • Authors: Stefano Onorati, Nabil Babu, & William Cook
  • Project Type: School

Audible Shapes

3D Generative Music Visualizer | April 2016

Audible Shapes is a 3D generative music visualizer. It allows the user to create a unique soundscape and manipulate objects within a 3D space. These objects will then generate sound and in combination create a piece of music or sound art. The user will simply use their hands for creating the objects, giving them a sense of familiarity and simplicity. The mixture of sound and interaction will create an immersive and enjoyable environment for anyone to create music.

This was my final project for the Digital Media program at York University. Victor Zohni and I spent the entire school year working on this from start to finish, with the main goal of allowing anyone to play music just by using their hands regardless of what musical experience they have or lack in.

TOOLS USED: Unity3D, C# scripting, Max/MSP, Leap Motion

  • Authors: Stefano Onorati & Victor Zohni
  • Project Type: School

DisPerSion Lab

Web Design | May 2016

Founded in 2015 by Doug van Nort, the DisPerSion Lab is a research lab for the Digital Media program at York University. It is dedicated to research-creation projects which examine questions surrounding instrumental and gestural expression, embodied perception, time consciousness and performative agency in the context of envisioning new forms of interdisciplinary creative practice.

The backbone of the site was previously built using Weebly, so my main focus for this project was to customize the front-end design of the site. I had discussed with Doug van Nort as to what colour scheme to use and what design changes to make. I also implemented my own code for creating the People page, which included the style of each bio/image combination and the behaviour of opening and closing bios on this page through Javascript. As well, the page was customized to become responsive, changing how many images to display per line.

TOOLS USED: HTML5, CSS3, Javascript, Weebly

  • Author: Stefano Onorati
  • Project Type: Business
